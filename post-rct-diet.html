After Root Canal Treatment, it is very important to be careful about what you eat and drink for the first few days. Eating the wrong foods can damage the temporary filling or crown and trigger new pain or sensitivity.

⏱️ Phase 1 — First 24 Hours (Most Critical)

The local anaesthetic wears off within 2–4 hours after RCT. While numbness persists, do not eat anything — you may accidentally bite your cheek or tongue without realising it.

✅ Kha sakte ho

🧊
Thanda paani ya lukewarm paani — slowly
🥛
Thanda doodh — soothing hota hai
🍦
Plain ice cream or yogurt — soft and cool
🍵
Lukewarm soup — avoid very hot
🍌
Mashed banana — no chewing needed

❌ Strictly Avoid

🔥
Koi bhi garam cheez — chai, daal, sabzi
🍗
Hard ya chewy khaana — roti, meat
🌶️
Spicy ya masaledar khaana
🥤
Cold drinks, juice — sugar + acid
🍫
Toffee, chocolate, chewing gum
📅 Phase 2 — Day 2 se Day 7 (Healing Week)

During this period, there is inflammation around the treated tooth. Eat only soft food and hamesha doosri taraf se chabaao.

✅ Safe khana

🥣
Dalia, khichdi — well cooked, mushy
🍚
Boiled rice — thoda geela rakho
🥚
Boiled egg, scrambled egg
🥣
Curd (dahi) — plain, no achari
🥕
Boiled vegetables — soft carrots, potato
🐟
Soft fish — boneless, boiled
🍌
Banana, chikoo, papaya — soft fruits

❌ Avoid These

🫓
Crusty bread, toast, rusk
🥜
Nuts, seeds, popcorn
🍎
Hard fruits — seb, nashpati seedha
🌽
Corn on cob, hard bhutta
🍬
Sticky candy, eclairs, caramel
🌶️
Mirchi, pickle, vinegar items
✅ Phase 3 — Week 2 Onwards (Normal Recovery)

If the crown has been placed, you can resume an almost normal diet. If you still have a temporary filling, be extra careful until the permanent crown is fitted.

✅ Crown lag gaya — almost normal

🍞
Regular roti-sabzi — normal khana
🍗
Soft chicken, dal-chawal
🥗
Most vegetables — normal cooking
😁
Normal smile, normal life!

⚠️ Temporary Filling In Place — Still Be Careful

🍬
Sticky foods can dislodge the temporary filling
🧊
Avoid extremes of hot or cold temperature
🥩
Hard meat — filling pe stress
📅
Get your crown placed soon — temporary filling is not permanent

🇮🇳 Indian Foods — Complete Safe List

Home-cooked food is ideal after RCT — here is a safe list of common Indian foods:

✅ Safe Indian Foods

🍚
Khichdi — best post-RCT meal
🥣
Dalia — soft, nutritious
🥛
Lassi / Chaas — cooling effect
🥣
Idli / Dosa — soft, no chewing
🍲
Dal soup — without jeera tadka initially
🎃
Lauki / Turai sabzi — soft vegetables
🧆
Paneer (soft) — no frying initially
🍌
Banana milkshake — no seeds/nuts

❌ Avoid These — Indian Foods

🫓
Rusk / Mathri — bahut hard
🌶️
Achar / Chutney — acid irritant
🧆
Fried samosa / pakoda — hard crust
🍬
Pan masala / Gutkha — infection risk
☕
Kadak chai — very hot, sugar
🌰
Chana, mungfali — hard to chew
🥘
Bhindi / Beans — seeds can get stuck
🔥
Tadka wali daal — first 2 days avoid
⚠️ Call us immediately if you notice: Severe pain that has not reduced after 3–4 days, increasing swelling, the temporary filling has come out, or you develop a fever. These signs indicate something needs to be checked promptly.
💡 Pro Tip: Do not eat for 30 minutes after RCT so the anaesthetic can settle. Always chew on the opposite side until the permanent crown is placed — this one simple rule prevents a great deal of discomfort.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How many days will I have pain after RCT?
Mild throbbing pain for the first 2–3 days is completely normal. It settles well with prescribed painkillers (Ibuprofen or Paracetamol). If severe pain persists beyond 4–5 days, call your dentist — occasionally a re-treatment session may be needed.
Can I drink tea or coffee after RCT?
Avoid hot tea completely for the first 24 hours. After that, you may drink lukewarm tea — nothing very hot. If your crown has not been placed yet, avoid adding sugar to tea to prevent decay in the temporary filling.
How soon after RCT can I eat normally?
If a permanent crown has been placed, you can return to almost normal eating within 1 week. If a temporary filling is in place, prefer soft foods until the crown is fitted. The longer you delay getting the crown, the greater the risk of re-infection or filling fracture.
Can I brush my teeth after RCT?
Yes, continue brushing — but be gentle near the treated tooth for the first 2–3 days. Use a soft bristle toothbrush. Stopping oral hygiene altogether increases infection risk.
The temporary filling has come out — what should I do?
Call your dentist immediately. Do not put anything inside the exposed cavity. Avoid biting on that tooth. While not a dental emergency, it should be repaired within 24–48 hours — bacteria can enter and re-infect the canal.
