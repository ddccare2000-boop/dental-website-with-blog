🪥 Correct Brushing Technique

Most people brush daily — but with the wrong technique. Learn the right method.

📌 India Research Finding: According to the IDA (Indian Dental Association), only 12% of Indians know the correct brushing technique. The remaining 88% simply scrub their teeth, which damages enamel over time.
1

Brush Angle — Hold at 45 Degrees

Hold the brush at a 45-degree angle along the gum line. Holding it flat or straight means bacteria between teeth and gums won't be removed. This is the Bass Method, which is globally recommended by dental experts.

2

Gentle Circular Motion — No Hard Pressure

Use short circular or gentle back-and-forth strokes. Never scrub hard — this wears down enamel. In India, hard brushing is the #1 cause of enamel erosion and tooth sensitivity.

3

The 2-Minute Rule — Full 120 Seconds

Research shows the average Indian brushes for only 45 seconds. Brush for a full 2 minutes — 30 seconds per quadrant (upper left, upper right, lower left, lower right).

4

Brush the Inner Surface (Tongue Side) Too

Most people only brush the outer surfaces. The inner tongue-side of teeth harbors far more bacteria, which is where most cavities actually begin.

5

Brush Your Tongue — It Is Essential

The white coating on your tongue is the single biggest source of bad breath. Clean it daily with a tongue scraper or the back of your brush — every morning.

6

Replace Your Brush — Every 3 Months

Frayed bristles on an old brush reduce cleaning effectiveness by 30%. The average Indian uses a brush for 8–9 months — this is incorrect and reduces oral hygiene significantly.

✅ DO — Good Habits

  • Brush morning and night — both times
  • Use a soft-bristle toothbrush
  • Use fluoride toothpaste
  • Do not eat after brushing at night
  • Use mouthwash after brushing
  • Replace brush when bristles start bending

❌ DON'T — Habits to Avoid

  • Never use a hard-bristle brush
  • Avoid aggressive side-to-side scrubbing
  • Do not brush immediately after eating (wait 30 min)
  • Never share your toothbrush with anyone
  • Do not brush only in the morning — night is essential
  • Do not use charcoal toothpaste daily — it erodes enamel

🧵 Flossing — Why It Is Essential

Only 2% of Indians floss regularly — this is a critical mistake

Research Fact: A toothbrush cleans only 60% of your tooth surfaces. The remaining 40% — the spaces between teeth — can only be cleaned by flossing. These spaces are the most common site for cavities and gum disease.
1

Take 18 Inches of Floss — Wrap Around Fingers

Use a long enough piece so you have a fresh section for each pair of teeth. Reusing the same section transfers bacteria back onto your teeth and gums.

2

Wrap in a C-Shape Around Each Tooth

Curve the floss into a C-shape around each tooth and slide it gently beneath the gumline. Snapping it straight up and down can cut and damage your gums.

3

Gentle Up-Down Motion — Never Force It

Slide slightly beneath the gumline but never force the floss. A little bleeding the first few times is completely normal — it will stop within 1–2 weeks as your gums become healthier.

💡 Best Alternative for India: If traditional floss is difficult to use, a Water Flosser (Oral Irrigator) is an excellent option — research shows it is as effective as traditional flossing. Interdental brushes are also increasingly popular and very easy to use.

😬 Bad Breath — Complete Causes & Solutions

Over 500 million people in India suffer from bad breath — and most causes are dental, not digestive

85–90% of bad breath cases originate inside the mouth — not the stomach. Understanding the real causes is the first step to a permanent solution:

🦠

Tongue Bacteria

70% of bad breath cases are caused by bacteria accumulated on the tongue

🦷

Cavities

An infected cavity or abscess produces odor-causing bacteria and gases

🔴

Gum Disease

Gingivitis and periodontitis are major contributors to persistent bad breath

💧

Dry Mouth

Reduced water intake lowers saliva production, allowing bacteria to multiply rapidly

🧅

Strong Foods

Garlic, onion, and strong spices cause temporary odor that passes within hours

🏥

Medical Reasons

Conditions like diabetes, acid reflux, or sinus infections can also cause breath odor

Permanent Solution — Step by Step

  • Use a Tongue Scraper Daily — Every Morning

    This single habit resolves 70% of bad breath problems. India has a rich tradition of tongue cleaning (neem datun) — and modern science fully confirms its effectiveness.

  • Drink Plenty of Water — 8+ Glasses a Day

    A dry mouth allows anaerobic bacteria to multiply rapidly. Water promotes saliva flow, which is the mouth's natural antibacterial defense system.

  • Chew Cardamom or Fennel Seeds — Natural Breath Fresheners

    A time-tested Indian remedy. Cardamom contains cineole — a compound that actively kills oral bacteria. It is more effective and natural than commercial gum or breath sprays.

  • Visit Your Dentist — Check for Cavities and Gum Disease

    If bad breath persists despite brushing and flossing, an underlying dental issue is likely the cause. Professional cleaning and treatment resolve 90% of chronic bad breath cases.

🚫 Common Dental Myths — Fact vs Fiction

Widespread dental misconceptions in India that can seriously harm your teeth

MYTH ❌

"Salt water gargling can heal a cavity"

FACT ✅

Salt water rinses are soothing for inflamed gums and can help with minor infections, but they cannot repair a cavity. Cavities require professional dental treatment — a filling or crown.

MYTH ❌

"Charcoal toothpaste naturally whitens teeth"

FACT ✅

Charcoal toothpaste is highly abrasive and wears down enamel over time. Regular use leads to increased yellowing and sensitivity long-term. Neither the ADA nor the Indian Dental Association recommend it.

MYTH ❌

"Baby teeth are temporary — no need to worry about cavities"

FACT ✅

Cavities in baby teeth can directly damage the permanent teeth developing underneath. Baby teeth remain in place until age 12 — their care is absolutely essential for healthy permanent teeth.

MYTH ❌

"Rinsing after brushing washes away the benefits of toothpaste"

FACT ✅

After brushing, simply spit out the excess toothpaste — do not rinse heavily with water. Fluoride needs to remain on your teeth to be effective in strengthening enamel and preventing decay.

MYTH ❌

"No pain means no dental problem — no need to see a dentist"

FACT ✅

Cavities cause no pain until they reach the nerve — by which point treatment becomes complex and expensive. A routine check-up every 6 months catches problems early. Prevention is always the most affordable form of dental care.

MYTH ❌

"Neem datun (twig) is better than a toothbrush — no need for a modern brush"

FACT ✅

Neem's antibacterial properties are scientifically proven — that part is true. However, neem twigs do not allow proper brushing technique and provide no fluoride protection. The ideal approach combines neem's benefits with a proper soft-bristle toothbrush and fluoride toothpaste.
