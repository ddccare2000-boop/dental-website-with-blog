What is Composite Restoration?

Composite restoration is a tooth-colored filling used to repair decayed, chipped, cracked or worn teeth. The material is made of a mixture of plastic and fine glass particles that bonds directly to the tooth, providing a natural look and strong hold.

In simple words: It's like a "cement" that matches your tooth color and fixes cavities or breaks in a single visit!

🦷 When is Composite Filling Needed?

  • Cavities / tooth decay
  • Chipped or cracked teeth
  • Worn down teeth
  • Replacing old silver (amalgam) fillings
  • Closing small gaps between teeth

After Composite Care Instructions

✅ DO's:
  • Brush twice daily
  • Floss gently around filling
  • Visit dentist every 6 months
  • Report if any roughness develops
❌ DON'Ts:
  • Avoid hard foods for 24 hours
  • Don't chew ice or hard objects
  • Avoid staining foods (coffee/tea) immediately
  • Don't ignore sensitivity (tell doctor)

⭐ Composite fillings last 5-7 years with good care.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does composite filling last?

A: With good care, 5-7 years. Regular checkups help extend life.

Q: Is composite filling painful?

A: No. Local anesthesia is given to numb the area. Procedure is painless.

Q: Can composite be used for front teeth?

A: Yes! It's perfect for front teeth because it matches natural color.

Q: Is it safe?

A: Yes, composite is mercury-free and completely safe.

Q: Can I eat immediately?

A: You can eat after 1-2 hours once anesthesia wears off. Avoid hard foods for 24 hours.

Types of Composite Materials We Use

Nanofill Composite

Ultra-fine nanoparticles create an extremely smooth surface. Best for front teeth where high esthetics and polish retention are required. Resists staining exceptionally well.

Packable (Condensable) Composite

Higher filler content makes it stiffer — ideal for posterior (back) teeth where strength and resistance to biting forces is critical. Excellent wear resistance.

Bulk-Fill Composite

Placed in a single layer up to 4mm thick — saves time while maintaining strength. Light-cured and used for deep cavities in molars. Reduced polymerization shrinkage.

📋 Composite vs Amalgam (Silver Filling) — Why Composite Wins

Feature ✨ Composite (Tooth-Colored) ⚫ Amalgam (Silver)
Appearance✅ Matches tooth color perfectly❌ Visible silver/grey color
Tooth Removal✅ Minimal — bonds to tooth❌ More tooth structure removed
Mercury Content✅ Mercury-free⚠️ ~50% mercury by weight
Durability✅ 7–10+ years with care10–15 years (but causes tooth cracks over time)
Sensitivity✅ Low post-procedure sensitivity⚠️ Higher initial temperature sensitivity

💡 Care Tips for Long-Lasting Composite Fillings

  • Avoid biting hard objects like ice, pen caps, or hard candy directly on the filling
  • Limit staining foods (tea, coffee, turmeric) especially in the first 48 hours
  • Brush gently with a soft-bristled toothbrush — aggressive brushing wears composite faster
  • Visit your dentist every 6 months — composites can be polished to restore their shine
  • If you grind teeth (bruxism), ask about a night guard — grinding can fracture composite
