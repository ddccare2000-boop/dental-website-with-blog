Denture Care Guide — Make Them Last Longer

🧼 Daily Cleaning

Remove and rinse after eating. Brush with a soft denture brush and mild soap — never toothpaste (too abrasive). Soak overnight in denture cleanser solution.

💧 Keep Them Moist

Never let dentures dry out — they may warp. Store in water or denture soaking solution when not wearing. Avoid hot water (causes distortion).

🏥 Regular Check-ups

Visit every 6–12 months. Gum tissue changes shape over time — dentures may need relining or replacement every 5–7 years to maintain proper fit.

⚠️ Avoid These

Whitening toothpaste (scratches surface), bleach (weakens denture), very hard/sticky foods, and DIY repair kits (can cause permanent damage).

💬 Frequently Asked Questions

How long to adjust to new dentures?
Most patients adapt within 4–8 weeks. Initial soreness, excess saliva, and altered speech are normal and improve with time.
Can I sleep with dentures?
Dentists recommend removing them at night to give gums time to rest and prevent bacterial buildup under the denture base.
Can dentures be repaired if cracked?
Yes! Most cracks and broken teeth in dentures can be repaired professionally. Bring both pieces immediately — don't use DIY adhesives.
