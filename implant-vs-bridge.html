Dental Implant
vs Dental Bridge

Which tooth replacement option is right for you? A clear, honest, side-by-side comparison — cost, durability, procedure, and more.

🔩

Dental Implant

A titanium screw placed in the jawbone — functions and looks like a natural tooth. Independent, permanent solution.

🦷

Dental Bridge

An artificial tooth anchored by crowns on adjacent teeth — bridges the gap without surgery. Proven, affordable solution.

🎯 Who Should Choose What?

🔩 Choose Implant If...

  • You want the most permanent solution
  • You value bone preservation long-term
  • Adjacent teeth are healthy — don't want them damaged
  • You have adequate bone volume (or can graft)
  • Budget allows for higher upfront investment
  • You're under 65 and in good general health
  • You want zero maintenance hassle (floss threaders etc.)
  • Only one or two teeth are missing with gaps between them

🦷 Choose Bridge If...

  • You need a faster solution (1–2 weeks)
  • Budget is limited (significantly lower cost)
  • Adjacent teeth already need crowns anyway
  • You have insufficient bone and don't want surgery
  • Medical conditions prevent implant surgery (uncontrolled diabetes, blood thinners)
  • Older patient with multiple missing teeth in a row
  • You want to avoid any surgical procedure
  • Temporary solution while planning implants later

Abutment Placement
Connector attached to implant after healing.
Final Crown
Custom zirconia/PFM crown cemented. Complete!

2 Bridge Steps

Consultation & X-Ray
Assess adjacent teeth strength and design the bridge.
Tooth Preparation
Adjacent teeth shaped and reduced under anesthesia — permanent change.
Impressions & Lab
Molds taken, bridge fabricated in dental lab (~1 week).
Temporary Bridge
Worn while permanent bridge is made.
Final Bridge Cemented
Permanent bridge fitted and checked for bite. Complete in ~2 visits!

❓ Common Questions

Can I get an implant after a bridge?

Yes! Many patients get bridges first and upgrade to implants later when budget allows. The bridge is removed, and if bone is adequate, implant placement proceeds normally.

Does bridge damage adjacent teeth permanently?

Yes, the enamel is permanently removed from adjacent teeth (abutments). This is why implants are preferred if those teeth are healthy — bridges are better when abutment teeth already need crowns.

Is implant surgery painful?

The surgery itself is painless with local anesthesia. Post-surgery discomfort is usually mild — managed with pain medication for 2–3 days. Most patients return to normal activity the next day.

Which lasts longer — implant or bridge?

Implants last 15–25+ years (often a lifetime), while bridges typically need replacement after 8–15 years. Long-term, implants are more cost-effective despite higher upfront cost.

Which is better for multiple missing teeth?

For 3+ consecutive missing teeth, an implant-supported bridge (2 implants supporting a multi-unit bridge) combines the bone-preservation benefits of implants with reduced cost vs. individual implants for each tooth.
