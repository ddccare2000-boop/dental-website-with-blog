Toothache strikes without warning — at night, on a holiday, or when the dentist is closed. These 5 home remedies will provide temporary relief. Remember: these are not a cure, only pain management — you must see a dentist to address the root cause.

⚠️ Read This First: If toothache is accompanied by facial swelling, fever, or difficulty breathing — this is a dental emergency. Go to your dentist or a hospital immediately. The infection may be life-threatening if left untreated.
1

🧄 Garlic — The Most Powerful Natural Remedy

India's #1 traditional remedy — and scientifically validated

✅ Science Approved

Garlic contains Allicin — a powerful natural antibacterial and anti-inflammatory compound. Research shows it is effective against dental bacteria, including Streptococcus mutans, the primary bacterium responsible for tooth decay.

🔪 How to Use:

  1. Take 1–2 fresh, raw garlic cloves
  2. Lightly crush or chew until the juice is released
  3. Place directly on the painful tooth — hold for 2–3 minutes
  4. Repeat 2–3 times per day
  5. Rinse your mouth afterwards to reduce the smell
⚠️ Garlic may cause gum irritation in some people. Do not hold for more than 5 minutes. Remove immediately if burning increases.
2

🧂 Salt Water Rinse — The Classic Remedy

Grandmother's remedy — and doctor recommended too

✅ Science Approved

A warm salt water rinse is a proven antiseptic solution. It reduces gum inflammation, decreases bacteria, and promotes wound healing. The ADA (American Dental Association) officially recommends this remedy.

🥄 How to Prepare:

  1. 1 glass of lukewarm water (warm, not hot)
  2. Dissolve ½ teaspoon of salt in it
  3. Rinse vigorously for 30 seconds
  4. Spit it out — do not swallow
  5. Repeat 3–4 times a day — including after meals
⚠️ Do not use too much salt — a concentrated solution will irritate the gums rather than soothe them. ½ tsp per glass is sufficient.

🌿 Clove — The Dentist's Secret

Dentists themselves use clove oil-based products in clinical procedures

✅ Dentist Approved

Clove contains Eugenol — a natural anaesthetic and antibacterial compound. Dentists use eugenol-based preparations in clinical procedures. At home, whole cloves or clove oil provide temporary numbing of dental pain.

🌿 How to Use (3 Methods):

  1. Whole clove: Place 1 clove next to the painful tooth and press gently. Hold for 15–20 minutes.
  2. Clove oil: Apply 2–3 drops on a cotton ball and press on the painful area (do not apply directly to the gum).
  3. Clove powder: Mix a small amount of powder with water to form a paste — apply to the affected area.
⚠️ Do not apply clove oil directly to the gums or soft tissue in large amounts — it can cause a chemical burn. Always apply via a cotton ball.
4

🧊 Cold Compress — Ice Pack

Effective for both swelling and pain — simple and instant

✅ Science Approved

A cold compress constricts blood vessels, reducing both swelling and nerve signal intensity. It is especially useful when the face is slightly swollen or when pain follows trauma to the tooth or jaw.

🧊 How to Apply Correctly:

  1. Wrap ice cubes in a cloth or place in a zip-lock bag
  2. Never apply ice directly to skin or gum — frostbite risk
  3. Hold on the outside of the cheek on the affected side for 15–20 minutes
  4. Take a 15-minute break and repeat
  5. Can be repeated 4–5 times per day
⚠️ If your tooth is highly sensitive to cold and cold makes the pain worse — do not use a cold compress. A warm compress will be more suitable for you.
5

💊 OTC Painkillers — Fastest Relief

When natural remedies are not enough — safe over-the-counter options

✅ Medically Proven

Over-the-counter painkillers available at home provide the fastest and most reliable relief. Ibuprofen (Brufen, Combiflam) is also anti-inflammatory — it addresses both pain and swelling. Paracetamol (Crocin, Calpol) is the safer option if you have a stomach problem.

💊 Safe Usage Guide:

  1. Ibuprofen 400mg (Brufen) — take after food, every 6–8 hours
  2. Paracetamol 500mg (Crocin) — safer option, every 4–6 hours
  3. Do not take both together for a higher dose — this is dangerous
  4. Do not take for more than 3 days without consulting a dentist
  5. Never place aspirin directly on the tooth — this is an old myth that burns the gum tissue
⚠️ If you have a stomach problem or ulcer, avoid Ibuprofen. If you are pregnant, consult a doctor before taking any medication.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Does placing aspirin directly on the tooth relieve pain?
No — this is a common myth that is actually harmful. Placing an aspirin tablet directly on the gum causes a chemical burn because aspirin is acidic. This makes the pain worse, not better, and can cause painful oral ulcers. Always swallow pain medication with water — never apply it topically.
Why does toothache feel worse at night?
Toothache often feels more intense at night because when you lie down, blood pressure in the head and jaw increases, putting more pressure on an already inflamed nerve. Also, there are no daytime distractions to divert your attention. Elevating your head slightly while sleeping can provide modest relief. The real solution is dental treatment — see your dentist as soon as possible.
Does whisky or alcohol help with toothache?
Applying a small amount of alcohol (on a cotton pad) provides very minimal temporary numbing — clove oil (eugenol) actually works far better and is safer. However, both are temporary measures only. Drinking alcohol to relieve toothache is not recommended — it does not effectively numb dental pain and can interfere with any medication your dentist may need to prescribe. See a dentist promptly for proper treatment.
How long can I rely on home remedies for toothache?
Home remedies are reliable for a maximum of 2–3 days only. If pain persists beyond 3 days — even if temporarily reduced by home remedies — the underlying cause has not been addressed. Continuing to delay treatment allows the infection or decay to progress deeper, making treatment more complex and expensive. A problem that needed a simple filling today may need a root canal next week if ignored.
