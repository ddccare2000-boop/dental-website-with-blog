What is Post & Core?

When a tooth is badly broken and there is not enough structure left to place a crown, Post & Core is a procedure where a small pin (post) is placed inside the root of the tooth to build it up. Then a crown is placed over it.

In simple words: Just like an iron rod is used to strengthen a weak wall, this treatment strengthens a broken tooth from the inside.

🔄 How is it done?

1. Examination

Doctor takes an X-ray to check if the root is strong enough.

2. Post Placement

A small pin (post) is fixed inside the root of the tooth.

3. Core Buildup

A core is prepared over the post to support the crown.

4. Crown Fixing

A new crown is placed — your tooth is now strong again!

Types of Posts Used in Dentistry

🔩 Prefabricated Metal Post

Made of stainless steel or titanium. Cost-effective and widely used. Placed directly into the root canal space. Available in various sizes to fit different canals.

🪄 Fiber Post (Glass Fiber)

The modern gold standard. White, tooth-colored, and flexible like natural dentin — reducing root fracture risk. Excellent esthetics for front teeth. Our preferred choice.

🏗️ Cast Post & Core

Custom fabricated in a lab for very severely damaged teeth. Provides maximum strength and precise fit when standard prefabricated posts aren't adequate.

⚠️ Post & Core vs Direct Buildup — What's the Difference?

When very little tooth structure remains after root canal treatment, your dentist must decide between two approaches:

Post & Core (recommended when <50% tooth remains)

  • Post anchors into the root for retention
  • Core builds up missing tooth structure
  • Required when crown has too little to grip
  • Significantly stronger long-term result

Direct Core Buildup (when 50%+ tooth remains)

  • Composite material used to build up tooth
  • No post needed — existing walls retain buildup
  • Faster and less expensive
  • Suitable for less severely damaged teeth

Our protocol: We use digital X-ray analysis to determine the best option for each individual tooth — never a one-size-fits-all approach.

✅ After Your Post & Core — What to Expect

  • Mild sensitivity for 24–48 hours — normal, managed with painkillers if needed
  • Avoid hard/crunchy food on the treated tooth until crown is placed
  • Crown placement is scheduled within 1–2 weeks — don't delay!
  • The post & core itself is permanent — only the crown may need replacement over decades
  • Regular checkups every 6 months to monitor the crown and gum health
